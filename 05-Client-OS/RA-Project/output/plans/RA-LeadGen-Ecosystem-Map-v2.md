## CORE THESIS

The Rolland Assurances Lead Gen Department is a single, unified acquisition machine
built on the SLM (Straight-Line Marketing) framework. It is not three parallel systems
running alongside each other — it is one system with distinct layers, each governing
a specific function.

```
SLM = The Operating System (the machine itself)
CAI = The Execution Toolkit (apps running on the OS — micro-level production)
Google Ads API = The Deployment Interface (the CLI that pushes the machine live)
Intelligence System = The Nervous System (external sensing + internal feedback)
```

The machine has one purpose:

> Convert strangers with a problem into qualified phone calls for Rolland Assurances,
> at a cost that makes the business profitable and scalable.

Every component in this document exists to serve that purpose. If it doesn't, it
doesn't belong here.

## THE TWO FOUNDATIONS

Before any system runs, two things must exist and be locked:

### Foundation 1 — The Two Offers

| Offer | Landing Page | Target | Problem Solved |
|-------|-------------|--------|----------------|
| Mutuelle Santé Sénior | /landing/sante-senior | Seniors 55+ | Overpaying for health insurance, insufficient reimbursements, rate increases |

These pages are the only conversion points. All traffic, all content, all ad spend
flows to one of these two URLs. Nothing else matters until someone calls.

### Foundation 2 — The Two Avatars

These are the irreducible psychological profiles that govern ALL messaging decisions.

#### Avatar A — Le Retraité Surpayé (Senior 55+)

```
WHO:      Retiree, 60–75 years old, fixed income, received a rate increase letter
PAIN:     Paying too much for coverage that doesn't reimburse what they actually need
          (dental, optical, hearing aids). Feels trapped. Doesn't know they can switch.
FEAR:     Losing coverage during a switch. Being sold something they don't understand.
DESIRE:   Pay less, get the same or better coverage. Talk to a real human, not a chatbot.
TRIGGER:  Rate increase notice (Loi Châtel window), dentist bill, prescription costs.
LANGUAGE: "J'en ai marre de payer autant", "c'est trop cher pour ce que c'est",
          "je sais pas comment m'y prendre"
3 TENS:   Product certainty = best deal available. Person trust = advisor who is on my side.
          Company trust = legitimate, established broker, not a scam.
```

#### Avatar B — L'Artisan BTP Bloqué (Trades Professional)

```
WHO:      Self-employed contractor, 28–55 years old. Electrician, mason, roofer,
          plumber. Has a job site waiting or just started their activity.
PAIN:     Can't start work without the attestation. Time is literally money.
          May have been refused by another insurer. May not know what they need.
FEAR:     Job site delay. Legal liability. Getting ripped off by a broker.
DESIRE:   Get the paperwork fast. Pay a fair price. Deal with someone who knows BTP.
TRIGGER:  New job site contract. Client demanding certificate. Renewal coming up.
          Previous insurer refused or raised price dramatically.
LANGUAGE: "J'ai un chantier qui attend", "on m'a refusé ailleurs",
          "j'ai besoin d'une attestation rapidement"
3 TENS:   Product certainty = right coverage, legally compliant. Person trust = expert
          in BTP, not a generalist. Company trust = handles complex profiles, won't
          abandon me.
```

### Sub-System 1 — SLM Market Intelligence (External)

**Purpose:** Understand the market before spending a single euro. Know what
competitors are doing, what the avatars are searching for, what language they use,
what pain points are underserved. This is the intelligence that shapes the inputs
to the machine.

**Cadence:** Deep research at launch. Quarterly refresh. Triggered review when CPL
degrades or a new competitor appears.

```
INPUT SOURCES:
├── Competitor Analysis
│   ├── Who is advertising on our target keywords?
│   ├── What are their ad angles? (headlines, descriptions, CTAs)
│   ├── What landing pages are they using? (offer structure, Three Tens audit)
│   ├── Where are the gaps? (angles they're NOT covering = our opportunity)
│   └── Tools: Google Ads Auction Insights, SemRush/SpyFu, manual SERP scraping
│
├── Avatar Research
│   ├── What exact language do Seniors use when searching? (verbatim forum quotes)
│   ├── What exact language do Artisans use? (professional forums, Reddit BTP)
│   ├── What are their actual fears about switching insurance?
│   ├── What objections do they have before calling?
│   └── Sources: Google forums, Que Choisir, forum-assurance.com, BTP communities
│
├── Market Demand Signals
│   ├── Google Keyword Planner: search volume trends per keyword
│   ├── Seasonality mapping: when does search spike? (Châtel window = Nov–Jan)
│   ├── Emerging terms: new regulations, new products entering market
│   └── Geographic demand: which regions search more for each product?
│
└── Search Intent Mapping
    ├── Informational queries → exclude from paid, use for content
    ├── Navigational queries → competitor brand terms (bid or exclude)
    ├── Commercial queries → priority targeting
    └── Transactional queries → highest bid, best ad, best landing page
```

**Output:** Updated Avatar Profiles, refined Keyword Strategy, new Ad Angles,
Competitive Gap Report, Seasonal Calendar.

### Sub-System 2 — Performance Feedback Loop (Internal)

**Purpose:** Study and analyze OUR OWN execution. Look at what the campaigns are
producing, identify what is working and what isn't, and generate precise directives
to change the inputs in order to achieve the desired results.

**Cadence:** Weekly review minimum. Daily check during first 30 days. Monthly deep audit.

```
INPUT SOURCES (What We Measure):
├── TRAFFIC LAYER
│   ├── Impressions (are we showing up?)
│   ├── CTR — Click-Through Rate (is the hook working?)
│   ├── CPC — Cost Per Click (is the market expensive? are we bidding right?)
│   └── Quality Score (is Google happy with relevance?)
│
├── CONVERSION LAYER
│   ├── Call volume (are people calling?)
│   ├── Call duration (are they qualified? ≥60s = qualified)
│   ├── Call rate from click (is the ad-to-call path working?)
│   └── Call time distribution (when do people call? validates ad schedule)
│
├── ECONOMICS LAYER
│   ├── CPL — Cost Per Lead / Qualified Call
│   ├── CPA — Cost Per Acquisition (after Rolland's close rate)
│   ├── LTGP:CAC ratio (is the unit economics healthy?)
│   └── Budget utilization (are we spending the full budget? over/under pacing?)
│
├── CONTENT LAYER
│   ├── Ad-level CTR (which creative wins?)
│   ├── Headline A/B performance (which angle resonates?)
│   ├── Ad group performance (which intent group converts best?)
│   └── Keyword-level data (which keywords drive qualified calls?)
│
└── CLOSE LAYER (Rolland's input required)
    ├── Leads contacted vs leads received
    ├── Close rate per segment
    ├── Average contract value per segment
    └── Time-to-close (how fast does a call become a contract?)
```

**Processing Logic — The 3-Layer Diagnostic:**

Before changing anything, run the 3-layer diagnostic to identify WHERE the
breakdown is. Changing the wrong layer wastes time and money.

```
LAYER 1 — HOOK FAILURE (The ad didn't earn attention)
  Signal:   CTR < 3%
  Cause:    Headlines not relevant, not stopping, not triggering the avatar's pain
  Fix:      Rewrite headlines using SLM 4-Second Rule + Avatar language
  Owner:    Aura (cai-hook-engineer) + SLM Persuasion Core

LAYER 2 — BODY FAILURE (The landing page didn't convert interest into intent)
  Signal:   CTR decent (>5%) but call rate low (<20%)
  Cause:    Three Tens not satisfied — product, person, company trust not built
  Fix:      SLM audit of landing page. Rebuild with Three Tens + Threshold sequence
  Owner:    CEO (SLM framework) + Coda (content structure)

LAYER 3 — CTA FAILURE (They reached the button but didn't act)
  Signal:   Call rate low despite landing page engagement
  Cause:    Action threshold not cleared. Too much friction, not enough urgency
  Fix:      Rewrite CTA section. Add urgency triggers. Reduce friction.
  Owner:    Aura (hooks/CTA copy) + SLM Threshold system
```

**Output:** Weekly Optimization Directive with:
- What to kill (pause ads, keywords, or groups)
- What to keep (leave unchanged, let it run)
- What to scale (increase budget or bid)
- What to test next (new angle, new keyword, new ad)

## AUTOMATION LAYER
### (The Google Ads API — Deployment Interface)

The Google Ads API script is NOT a standalone pillar. It is the deployment interface
that sits between the Execution Engine and the live Google Ads account. It automates
what was previously done manually in the Google Ads UI.

```
CURRENT STATE (v1 — create_rolland_campaigns.py)
├── Creates campaigns, ad groups, keywords, ads via CLI
├── Pushes complete campaign structure in one command
└── Status: Built. Needs credentials and phone number to deploy.

NEXT STATE (v2 — planned)
├── Reads performance data from Google Ads API
├── Generates weekly performance report (CPL, CTR, call volume)
├── Flags alarm signals automatically (CPL > target, CTR < 3%)
└── Proposed: auto-pauses underperforming ad groups after threshold

FUTURE STATE (v3 — advanced)
├── Auto-adjusts bids based on performance rules
├── Adds negative keywords from search term report automatically
└── A/B test rotation management
```

**Position in the ecosystem:** The API script is a tool inside Layer 2 (Traffic System).
It accelerates deployment and reporting. It does not change the strategy — it executes it.

### Meta-Rule: Felix's Scaling Gates

```
GATE 1 — Validation Gate
  Condition: ≥30 conversions on a campaign
  Action: Switch from "Maximize Conversions" to "Target CPA" bidding

GATE 2 — Scaling Gate
  Condition: CPL < target × 0.8 (consistently over 2 weeks)
  Action: Increase budget 20–30%

GATE 3 — Kill Gate
  Condition: CPL > target × 1.5 (after 30 days)
  Action: Pause group, run 3-layer diagnostic, revise or kill

GATE 4 — Profitability Gate
  Condition: LTGP:CAC ratio must exceed 3x before any significant scale
  Formula: (Commission × Close Rate × LTV multiplier) ÷ CPL > 3
```
