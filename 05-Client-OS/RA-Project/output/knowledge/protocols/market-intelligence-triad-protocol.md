# Protocol: The Competitive Intelligence Triad
## Strategy: SLM Market Intelligence (Sub-system 1)

**Framework:** Triad of Second-Order Thinking (03), Incentive Systems (77), and Surface Area (47).
**Objective:** Replace "Guessing" with Data-Driven Certainty for Rolland Assurances.

---

### Phase 1: Surface Area (47) — "The Data Net"
**Purpose:** Cast a wide net to capture signals from the market without manual overload.

1.  **Direct Search Capture:** Weekly SERP scrape for "Mutuelle Santé Sénior" and "Garantie Décennale". Record the top 5 advertisers.
2.  **Asset Inventory:** Archive their headlines, landing page URLs, and CTAs.
3.  **Avatar Verbatim Harvest:** Weekly pull from "Que Choisir" forums, BTP Reddit, and general insurance forums. Look for "I am frustrated because..." language.
4.  **Automation Trigger:** Use `api_examples/` logic to monitor keywords and changes in SERP results.

### Phase 2: Incentive Systems (77) — "The Why Diagnostic"
**Purpose:** Decode why competitors are successful. Never blindly copy.

For every competitor asset captured in Phase 1:
1.  **The Incentive Test:** Ask: "Is this competitor optimized for volume (low-friction, mass-market) or high-ticket (authority-based, expert)?"
2.  **System Mapping:** If they use a specific landing page structure, identify if it’s designed to *qualify* leads (long form) or *capture* leads (short form).
3.  **Gap Identification:** Find where their incentive structure *fails* the Avatar (e.g., they optimize for lead volume, resulting in poor-quality support. We optimize for *call duration*).

### Phase 3: Second-Order Thinking (03) — "The Antithesis"
**Purpose:** Architect the superior response to gain market share.

For every insight from Phase 2:
1.  **The "And Then What?" Chain:**
    *   *Competitor Action:* Aggressive "Low Price" ads.
    *   *First Order:* We could also lower prices.
    *   *Second Order:* Margin compression for us + distrust from the senior avatar (they fear low price = low quality/service).
    *   *Third Order:* We build a "Certainty" offer (Expertise + Trust) that commands a premium.
2.  **Protocol Output:** Generate a Weekly Optimization Directive (Direct input for Trace).
3.  **Directive Format:**
    *   **Keep:** What is working for competitors that we must adopt as a baseline.
    *   **Kill:** What "guesswork" are we doing that fails the Second-Order test?
    *   **Scale:** What "Certainty" messaging can we push harder based on competitor gaps?
