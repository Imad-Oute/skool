### 1.1 The Three Tens Framework

| Ten | What It Is | Goal |
|-----|-----------|------|
| 1st Ten | Product | Absolute certainty — "best thing since sliced bread" |
| 2nd Ten | You (The Person) | Trust + rapport at the highest level |
| 3rd Ten | Your Company | Trust + connection with the entity |

**Key Facts about the Three Tens:**
- To successfully sell, it's essential to ensure that prospects trust and connect with all three key aspects: the product, yourself, and the company.
- Depending on the nature of what you're selling, the importance of these elements may vary. For example, when selling an information product, you need to establish trust in both the expert delivering the content and the company providing the training.
- Some markets require you to emphasize all three aspects, while others may focus on just one or two.
- Great copywriters often achieve success by instinctively satisfying these Three Tens — even if they aren't consciously aware of it.
- The reason long-form sales letters don't push for an immediate call to action is that they intuitively understand the need to build this trust first.
- In some cases there's no company to sell — like Jordan himself is the company; you buy the straight line system because of who Jordan Belfort is.

**Work on getting your Three Tens at the same time you're raising up their pain threshold and lowering their action threshold.**

---

### 1.2 The Threshold System

**Pain Threshold**
- How much pain the prospect currently feels about their problem
- It may take some work on your part to get your prospect to feel the pain and take action

**Action Threshold**
- The unconscious set point that somebody has to be motivated to take action
- **Rule:** Never ask for a CTA until you've passed the buyer's action threshold and built all Three Tens
- You should not ask for an action until you get the Three Tens and satisfy the action threshold
- In a sales letter you will never see the buy button at the top; in a VSL the buy button only appears when the video gets close to the end
- **You can't ask for a CTA until you've passed the buyer's action threshold**

**Goal:** "Tip the Scales" — make the positives outweigh the negatives. Knock out the negatives and build up the positives.

| The Negatives | The Positives (Your Three Tens) |
|---------------|--------------------------------|
| Pain threshold | Product |
| Action threshold | You |
| | Your Company |

### 1.3 Communication Architecture

- **Words:** 9% of persuasion
- **Tonality:** 45% of persuasion
- **Body Language:** 46% of persuasion

> NLP says: Communication = 9% word + 46% Body Language + 45% Tonality

**If you want to influence & persuade them and close the deal, you must establish:**
1. That you care about them
2. That you are trust-worthy

They must see you in this way and believe it — and the only way to do this is not through saying words, but through the way your truly connect on an unconscious and emotional level. This happens unconsciously and through the use of effective Tonality.

**The 4-Second Rule:** You have the first 4 seconds to establish 3 things. If you don't, you will have a very very difficult time taking this person down the road.

1. Sharp as a tack
2. Enthusiastic as hell
3. An expert in your field

The reason this is important: when people think that about you they're like "WOW, this is a person worth listening to." And the reason they're listening is because they believe this is a person who can help them achieve their goals.

### 1.4 The Straight Line Structure

**The Offline SLP Method — from Open to Close:**

```
OPEN ————————————————————————————— CLOSE
      1. Establishing Rapport
      2. Gathering Intelligence
```

Within the boundaries, we are doing two things while moving forward on the straight line:
1. Establishing Instant Massive Rapport through Tonality and Body Language
2. Using That Rapport to Gather Intelligence, find out what the prospect truly needs, and determine their true desires

**The 6 Stages:**

1. **Opening** — Introduction + establish the 3 things in the first 4 seconds
2. **Qualifying** — Questioning: starting from the Big Picture into Details
3. **Main Body** — Get YOUR Three Tens + raise up their pain threshold & lower their action threshold
4. **Looping** — Answering their objections and turning it to the Close
5. **Closing**
6. **Follow-Up**

**Key rules:**
- Based on how they respond and interact with you, you will either shorten your presentation or lengthen it and loop back.
- In the beginning of the straight line, especially in the qualifying process, you need to make sure you're listening effectively. Your prospect needs to see and feel that you absolutely care about what they are saying — and the way to do this is through Tonality.
- When the prospect speaks about something entirely irrelevant to the sale, you must guide them back within the boundaries. Do this by asking them questions relevant to the sale.

### 2.1 Copywriting System (Module 3)

**Core Principle:** A script is the well for the essence of a perfect sale. Copywriting is a sale — straight from open to close. There's a straight line, and you stay on that line. Craft a sales letter from this approach, and it will outperform anything done before.

> "Copywriting is salesmanship in print." Another way to view copywriting is as "salesmanship multiplied." While a typical sale is one-to-one — one salesperson conversing with one prospect — copywriting is one-to-many.

**Gary Halbert's Secret (the best copywriter in history):**
1. Go to the client's sales department
2. Identify the top salesperson
3. Record their sales pitch
4. Transcribe the recording
5. Craft the sales letter from the transcription
6. Modify and polish

By using this method, you're leveraging the most successful sales techniques of the top performers. Their approach has already proven to be effective in real sales situations, so your sales letter has a strong foundation built on real-world success.

#### The Copywriting Formula (6 Steps)

**Step 1 — Features**
Features are what's inside your product; it's what it consists of.

**Step 2 — Benefits**
Benefits is what makes your product's features so exciting and useful to your prospect. Ask yourself: "What do those features mean to my prospect?" — the answer is THE benefits.

**Step 3 — Story**
A story will help suck a prospect into your letter and make it impossible to stop reading.

*Story Formula:*
1. Problem
2. Why the problem could never be solved
3. Your magic solution
4. The results of your solution to the problem

The story should always further the sale. Write an emotional story.

**Step 4 — Summary**
Stack up the benefits for a big bang.

**Step 5 — Headline**
Remember Jordan's 4-second rule! (Sharp as a tack, enthusiastic as hell, and expert in your field.) Take a look at what's actually trending to help get ideas — use digg.com.

**Step 6 — CTA**
ASK FOR THE ORDER.

> The fastest way to become a kick-ass copywriter: pick up some of the greatest performing pieces of copy.
> A sales letter is nothing more than a great script modified slightly so that it makes sense.

#### The 6 Landing Pages You'll Ever Need

**1. Opt-in Page**

- Process: Attract Visitors → Present the Offer → Request Contact Info → Form Submission → Delivery of Offer → Lead Nurturing

- Key characteristics:
  - Compelling Headline

  - Benefits-Focused Content
  - Social Proof and Testimonials
  - Overcoming Objections
  - Call to Action (CTA)
  - Guarantees and Risk Reversal

**4. Content / Article / Pre-sell Page**
- Designed to provide valuable information, build trust, and gently guide visitors toward a conversion goal without directly selling

  - Educational Content
  - Indirect Promotion

  - Storytelling
  - Trust and Authority Building

**5. VSL (Video Sales Letter)**

  - Storytelling
  - Social Proof
  - Addressing Objections
  - Call to Action (CTA)

**6. Webinar Sign-up Page**
- Objective: get a sign-up
- Key Elements:
  - Headline (clear and engaging)
  - Subheadline (supporting text)
  - Webinar Details (date, time, duration, speaker info, agenda)

  - Mobile Responsiveness
  - Thank You Page (confirmation + next steps)

If you focus exclusively on increasing the value of your customer and don't care how your competitor buys their traffic — doesn't matter how aggressive they are with split testing and conversion rate obsession — because YOU'RE focusing on the 20% that controls the 80%. If you just found a way to increase the value of your customer, you're so far ahead of your competitor.

> Sales Funnels are the only practical way to increase the value of each customer and hence allow companies to spend more to acquire new customers. Focusing on building a kick-ass SLM Sales Funnel puts you ahead of your competitor. **The company with the best sales funnel always wins.**

#### The 6-Step SLM Funnel

```
Lead Capture → Sales Page (SLO) → Order Form → Upsell → Downsell → Thank You Page
```

| Step | Name | Purpose | Analogy |
|------|------|---------|---------|
| 1 | **Opt-in Bridge** | Capture email in exchange for lead magnet | Menu outside store with very interesting low-price offer |

| 6 | **Funnel Follow-Up** | Follow up with customers who haven't converted to the next step; put them into a follow-up series to increase the value of each prospect who enters the funnel | After-visit marketing |

**Your Perfect SLM Funnel:**
```
Lead Capture → Sales PG → Order Form → Up/Down Sell (optional) → Up (optional) → Down Sell (optional) → Thank You Page
```

#### Offline Looping Psychology

**Handling Objections and Tightening the Loop:**
- When you get an objection from the customer, you can't just ignore it and try to close them anyway. Instead, you answer their objection and you go backward and re-sell the Three Tens, then re-ask for the order.
- How they answer your re-ask (pay close attention to tonality and body language) determines if you need to loop again.
- As you loop, the loop gets more and more tighter — because you're talking less and less about the product and more and more about yourself, direct benefits to them about your integrity, how you're handling people, and what you can do for them beside this transaction over the long term.
- You never ever ever end a call in a bad way — be kind, good, and treat people with RESPECT even if they don't buy.

---

#### Looping Sequence

| Loop | Focus | Modality |
|------|-------|---------|
| Loop 1 | Re-sell the **Product** | Logic-based |
| Loop 2 | Re-sell **You** | Trust + personal connection |
| Loop 3 | Re-sell the **Company** | Credibility + authority |

**Rule:** Each loop gets tighter — less about the product, more about you and direct benefits.

> Create at least 5 sentences each about product, you, and your company. On every single paragraph, re-sell them emotionally and logically about one of the Three Tens. Remember: there is emotion AND logical certainty — you must get both.

- 5 sentences for re-selling the PRODUCT emotionally and logically
- 5 sentences for re-selling YOU emotionally and logically
- 5 sentences for re-selling your COMPANY emotionally and logically
- 5 sentences for asking for the order — emotional-based close
- 5 sentences for answering their objection with perfect tonality

> It's important to use an emotional-based close, which has more to do about the prospect seeing herself or himself using the product and feeling good in the future.

#### Objection Deflection Strategy

> When you ask for the order and they say "Let me think about it" — **YOU DO NOT ANSWER THEIR OBJECTION DIRECTLY**. You deflect their objection no matter what they say, and you handle that objection on your timing. If you answer their objection directly, you seem pushy.

**Script:**
> "I hear what you're saying, but let me ask you a question — does the idea make sense to you? Not a big deal, money aside..."

When they answer: if it's not an enthusiastic answer, you don't move forward — you loop again on re-selling your product.

#### Pixel Technology

**What is a Pixel?**
A pixel is a small piece of JavaScript code that you place on your website, which allows platforms like Facebook or Google to track user interactions on your site. The pixel records visitors' behavior (page views, purchases, button clicks) and sends this data back to the ad platform to help create targeted ad campaigns.

**Where to place it:** In the `<head>` section of your website HTML

**How it works:**
- The pixel fires (activates) when a user visits the page where it's placed
- This "firing" sends data back to your ad platform (Facebook or Google Ads)
- This allows you to track and retarget the user later

**Common Actions Tracked:**
- Page View: tracks when a visitor lands on a page
- Add to Cart: tracks when a visitor adds an item to their cart
- Purchase: tracks when a visitor completes a purchase
- Lead: tracks when a visitor submits a form
- Custom Events: any specific behavior that matters to your business

**How Pixels Enable Retargeting:**
1. Someone visits your sales page (tracked by the pixel) but doesn't purchase
2. The pixel sends that data to Facebook or Google
3. You create a Custom Audience based on this pixel data (e.g., "all users who visited the sales page but didn't hit the thank-you page")
4. This Custom Audience is used in your retargeting campaign to serve specific ads

**Setup process:**
- Step 1: Create pixel in your ad platform (get code snippet)
- Step 2: Install pixel on your website (paste in `<head>`)
- Step 3: In ad platform, go to Events Manager to verify the pixel is working
- Step 4: When creating ads, choose Custom Audience built from pixel data — the platform automatically uses pixel data for retargeting

## SYSTEM CONTEXT: THE OFFLINE vs. ONLINE METHOD

### The Offline Marketing Method
1. Write advertisement in newspaper with compelling headline and offer
2. Include CTA encouraging readers to call a toll-free or specific phone number
3. When reader calls: collect information (name, address, email) in exchange for a valuable report
4. Send promised report via physical mail or email
5. Follow-up:
   - Direct mail: personalized follow-up letter or postcard a few days later with exclusive discount
   - Phone call: follow-up call a week later to discuss the report, answer questions, offer assistance

### Direct Response Campaigns

**Key Characteristics:**
- Clear CTA: strong, specific calls to action ("Buy Now")
- Measurable Results: tracks immediate responses
- Targeted Audience: focuses on specific audience segments
- Sense of Urgency: time-sensitive offers prompt quick action

**Examples:**
- Email campaigns: discount codes with limited-time offers
- Social media ads: ads prompting ebook downloads for email sign-up
- TV/Radio ads: commercials encouraging immediate calls
- Direct mail: physical mail with coupons or special offers
- Online display ads: banners leading to landing pages

**Use Cases:**
- Common in e-commerce, retail, service-based businesses
- Drives quick sales, generates leads, builds immediate customer connections

### Brand Awareness Campaign
- Goal: increase visibility, awareness, and recognition of a brand
- Channels: social media, online advertising, PR, influencer marketing
- Example: a company launching a new logo or brand identity

### The Online Method
1. Website where we encourage clicking a link to get a report
2. Squeeze Page: encourage them to leave some information
3. Buy page
4. Thank you Page

**The Formula to Succeed Online:**
1. **WHO** is your target market? Use your Ideal Customer (Eben Pagan course)
2. **WHERE** are they?
3. **BAIT:** What's the kind of bait that makes them excited and attracts their attention?
4. **WHAT** do they want? What can we offer them? What can we sell them?
