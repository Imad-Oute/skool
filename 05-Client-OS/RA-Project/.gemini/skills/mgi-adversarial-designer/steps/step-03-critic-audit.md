# Step 3: Critic Audit

**Agent:** Critic 🎯  
**Objective:** Identify cognitive biases, logical fallacies, and structural blind spots in the plan.

---

## Your Task

You are the **Critic**. The Inversionist found structural failure modes. Your job is different — you audit the _reasoning_ behind the plan. Structural fixes can't save a plan built on flawed thinking.

### Cognitive Bias Scan

Check the plan against each bias category:

**Optimism & Overconfidence**

- [ ] **Planning fallacy**: Are timelines realistic or wishful?
- [ ] **Overconfidence**: Is success treated as more likely than evidence supports?
- [ ] **Illusion of control**: Does the plan assume more control than is realistic?

**Information Processing**

- [ ] **Confirmation bias**: Is the plan ignoring contradicting evidence?
- [ ] **Availability heuristic**: Is the plan overweighting recent/memorable data?
- [ ] **Anchoring**: Is the plan anchored to an initial number/idea that distorts everything else?

**Social & Incentive Biases**

- [ ] **Sunk cost**: Is the plan continuing something because of past investment, not future value?
- [ ] **Social proof**: Is the plan following what others are doing instead of first principles?
- [ ] **Authority bias**: Is the plan deferring to an authority whose incentives misalign with yours?

**Systemic Thinking Failures**

- [ ] **Local optimization**: Is the plan optimizing one part at the expense of the whole?
- [ ] **Short-termism**: Does the plan sacrifice long-term resilience for short-term performance?
- [ ] **Complexity underestimation**: Is the plan treating a complex system as a simple one?

### Logical Fallacy Scan

- [ ] **False dichotomy**: Is the plan treating a spectrum as a binary choice?
- [ ] **Correlation/causation**: Is the plan assuming X causes Y when it only correlates?
- [ ] **Scope creep logic**: Is the plan using success in a small context to justify a large one?
